How Does Split Schedule Work?

When parents agree to use Split Schedule, each parent will create a unique login and password for access to the site. Each parent will have access to shared scheduling information, a parenting journal to keep accurate records, and messaging capability.

  • Propose Schedule Changes

    Use Split Schedule's messaging program and calendar to propose scheduling changes to your co-parent. Approve or confirm your co-parent's proposed scheduling changes by simply clicking a box.

  • Reduce Conflicts

    By sharing a calendar you and your co-parent can more easily plan activities, vacations, and holiday parenting time without the worry of creating scheduling conflicts for your children.

  • Share Information

    Input your children's school and activity schedules into the Split Schedule calendar so you and your co-parent have access to the same information.

  • Keep Accurate Records

    Keep journal entries in the Split Schedule Parenting Journal about your children's calendar, and remove the "he-said, she-said" from your parenting case.

How do I use Split Schedule?

If you are the first parent to find Split Schedule, you will need to create an account for yourself and provide information so that we can automatically create a second account for your co-parent. If your co-parent has already created a Split Schedule account, you will be able to access the shared calendar by following the link in your email.
